# Acne Diagnostics Protocol

## Global Acne Grading System (GAGS)

The GAGS (Global Acne Grading System) is a standardized method for assessing acne severity:

### Scoring Method:
- **Score Range**: 0-44 points
- **Face Regions**: 6 areas (forehead, right cheek, left cheek, nose, chin, chest/back)
- **Lesion Types**:
  - Comedones (0-2 points per region)
  - Papules (0-3 points per region)
  - Pustules (0-3 points per region)
  - Nodules (0-4 points per region)

### Severity Levels:
- **0-18**: Mild acne
- **19-30**: Moderate acne
- **31-38**: Severe acne
- **39-44**: Very severe acne

### Lesion Differentiation:

**Pustules**:
- Superficial, pus-filled lesions
- White or yellow center
- Red, inflamed base
- Typically 2-5mm in diameter
- Surface-level, easily visible

**Nodules**:
- Deep, solid, painful lesions
- No visible pus
- Extends deep into dermis
- Typically 5-10mm or larger
- Hard to the touch, may feel like a knot

**Cystic Acne**:
- Deepest form of acne
- Large, painful, pus-filled
- Can cause scarring
- Often 10mm+ in diameter
- May have multiple heads or connections

### Diagnostic Criteria:
1. Count all visible lesions by type
2. Assign points per facial region
3. Sum total GAGS score
4. Classify severity level
5. Note distribution pattern (localized vs. widespread)
